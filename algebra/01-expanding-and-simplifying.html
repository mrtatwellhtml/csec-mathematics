Algebra 1: Expanding & Simplifying

In this lesson, you will practise removing brackets and simplifying expressions. This skill appears very often in exams and is usually the first step in longer questions.

Key Reminder

Everything inside the bracket must be multiplied.

Guided Example

Simplify:

5(2x − 3) − 2(3x + 4)

Step 1: Expand both brackets.

Step 2: Simplify fully.

Try This (Guided Practice)

Simplify:

3(4a − 2) − (5a − 7)

Step 1: Expand both brackets.

Step 2: Simplify fully.

Tip: Watch the minus sign in front of the second bracket. It changes every sign inside.

Practice (Exam Style)

1. Simplify: 2(x − 3) + 4(2x + 1)

2. Simplify: 6(3y − 5) − 2(4y + 1)

3. Simplify: -3(2p − 7) + 5(p + 4)

4. Simplify: 7 − 2(3x − 5)

5. Simplify: 4(a − 3) − 3(2a − 1)

Exam Reminder

If expanding is wrong, everything after it loses marks. Slow down, check signs, then simplify.
